Introduction


Poverty has remained stubbornly persistent, and the path out of it is rarely clear.  But solutions are not as far away as they might seem, though they are not always intuitive.

You will take the role of a family in rural India.  You will be making economic, health, and reproductive decisions to improve the well-being of your family.

Background


Clloquially known as “the Land of Kings”, Rajasthan is located in northern India and is the country's largest state. Beyond the shadows of historic forts and grand palaces that adorn cities like Jodhpur, roughly three-quarters of the Rajasthan’s population of 68 million people live in rural areas.

Your Story


In a small village, in the southern part of the state, Ajay and Sapna, are starting their life together. The marriage of Ajay, 19, and Sapna, 17, was arranged by their parents. Ajay has seven years of schooling and can read, while Sapna, who did not get past an elementary education, is illiterate. Like most of the rural population, they work as subsistence farmers, on the same four acres of land that Ajay inherited from his parents.

Taking On Jobs


You and your spouse plan on taking a few extra side jobs to earn money.  You can split your time and resources between tailoring, furniture making, and cooking.  You know that the more time you spends on one job, the more you can specialize and the more money you'll make per day worked.  But you also know that if you work on only one job, there's a lot of risk by putting "all your eggs into one basket", and if something goes wrong, you'll be in trouble.

Children


You know that it's about the time to start having kids.  However, you face a series of important decisions: how many children to have, who to send to school, who should stay home to work, and even whether or not to keep the children you conceive.

You will have to take account of how much money you have, the fact that you will have to save for a dowry for your daughters, and how much success you think your children will have in school.
